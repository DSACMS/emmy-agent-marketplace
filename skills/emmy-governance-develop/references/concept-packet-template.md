# Governance Concept Packet Template

Produce this packet when the human asks for a summary, handoff, next-step brief,
or when the concept is ready to move toward document drafting.

Do not fill unknowns with invented facts. Mark unknowns as open questions.

```markdown
## Governance Concept Packet

### Governance Need

[What problem, obligation, risk, or communication need this governance work
addresses.]

### Domain

[Security & Compliance / Open Source / Release & Comms / Unknown]

### Current Understanding

[Concise synthesis of what the human has said and what the agent has learned.]

### Relevant Knowledge Store Findings

[Page title, URL or page ID, finding, and freshness caveat. Use "No targeted
lookup performed" or "No relevant findings found" when appropriate.]

### Proposed Operating Model

[Current best version of how the process should work: triggers, steps, roles,
tools, outputs, approval path, evidence trail, and review cadence.]

### Decisions Made

- [Decision and rationale.]

### Assumptions

- [Assumption and how it should be verified.]

### Open Questions

- [Question, why it matters, and who likely needs to answer.]

### Risks Or Weak Spots

- [Risk or underdeveloped area, plus suggested mitigation or next inquiry.]

### Evidence Needed

- [Evidence source needed to make this auditable or draft-ready.]

### Draft-Skill Readiness

[Ready / Almost ready / Not ready]

[Explain what is missing before using `emmy-governance-doc-draft`.]
```

## Readiness Guide

- `Ready`: purpose, scope, authority, current process, roles, evidence, and
  review cadence are clear enough to draft.
- `Almost ready`: only one or two specific facts are missing.
- `Not ready`: the operating model, authority, scope, or approver remains
  unresolved.
